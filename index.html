Thesis

We acquire profitable niche vertical SaaS — not to optimize and exit, but to understand the markets they serve.

In the agentic era, an embedded position in a specialized market is worth more than the SaaS itself.

Most acquirers treat the asset as the end product — something to polish and resell. We treat it as a vantage point. Workflow data, behavioral telemetry, cross-customer patterns that no single company can see. That's not a feature set. That's a compounding advantage.

Phase 1
Systematize the Operator
Encode how we evaluate, integrate, and run acquired companies. Build institutional memory that compounds across deals.
Phase 2
Build the Intelligence Layer
Turn data exhaust into structured insight — supply-side, demand-side, and outcome signals that reveal the market.
Phase 3
Deploy into the Market
Extend into the adjacent labor the SaaS already serves. The product stops being a tool and starts being an operating layer.
