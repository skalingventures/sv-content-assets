Decision Upgrades

Q2 hiring: second AE vs. first RevOps hire

↓ Recommendation
Hire RevOps first. The pipeline exists but isn't converting efficiently. Adding another AE to a leaky funnel accelerates waste, not revenue. Revisit the second AE in Q3 after velocity metrics stabilize.
Decision
Should we allocate the Q2 headcount budget to a second AE or our first RevOps hire?
Options + Tradeoffs
Option A — Hire second AE. Adds quota capacity immediately but doesn't fix the conversion bottleneck. Risk: same pipeline velocity with more mouths to feed. Option B — Hire RevOps. Instruments the funnel, identifies where deals stall, and unblocks existing pipeline. Tradeoff: delays quota capacity by one quarter. Option C — Fractional RevOps consultant + second AE. Splits the difference but neither gets full commitment. Risk: consultant lacks context to move fast.
Evidence Signals
RevOps-to-AE Ratio Predicts Conversion; SaaS Benchmarks: NRR Median at 105%; Fed Holds Rates (buyer caution)
Assumptions
Pipeline coverage stays above 3.5x through Q2. The velocity problem is process friction, not lead quality.
What would change my mind
Pipeline coverage drops below 3x, indicating the problem is actually demand, not conversion.

Partner co-selling pilot: invest or defer?

↓ Recommendation
Run a 60-day pilot with the ERP integration partner. Cap investment at $15K and 20 hours of AE time. Measure CAC and deal size against direct channels.
Decision
Should we allocate budget to a partner co-selling pilot in Q2 or wait until direct channel efficiency improves?
Options + Tradeoffs
Option A — Run the pilot now while CPL is rising. Takes advantage of partner readiness. Tradeoff: splits AE attention during a conversion-critical quarter. Option B — Defer to Q3. Focus all energy on fixing direct conversion. Tradeoff: misses the window while the partner is motivated and CPL keeps climbing.
Evidence Signals
Partner-Led Growth Outpaces Paid Acquisition; BLS Services Hiring Goes Flat
Assumptions
The ERP partner has enough overlapping customers to generate at least 10 qualified introductions in 60 days.
What would change my mind
Partner produces fewer than 5 introductions in the first 30 days, suggesting the overlap thesis is wrong.

Acquisition target re-engagement: stale LOIs

↓ Recommendation
Re-engage the top two stale LOIs with updated pricing. Use the seller expectation reset as the opening. Structure earnouts tied to NRR thresholds.
Decision
Should we revisit deal conversations that stalled on valuation in 2024–25?
Options + Tradeoffs
Option A — Re-engage now. Sellers are resetting expectations by 15–25%. Tradeoff: restarting conversations takes deal team bandwidth. Option B — Wait for inbound. Sellers who are ready will come to us. Tradeoff: misses the reset window; PE competition is increasing (31% YTD).
Evidence Signals
Seller Expectation Reset in Sub-$5M ARR; PE Add-On Acquisitions Up 31% YTD; Vertical SaaS Exits Commanding Premium Multiples

At least one of the three stale targets has NRR above 110% and would pass current diligence standards.
